Cloud Computing: Infrastructure as a Service

Cloud üìÖ Updated: December 2024 ‚è±Ô∏è 15 min read

Cloud computing is the on-demand availability of computer system resources, especially data storage and computing power, without direct active management by the user.

Introduction

The cloud allows businesses to scale their infrastructure rapidly and pay only for what they use. The three main service models are IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS.

Service Models

  • IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service): Virtualized computing resources over the internet (e.g., AWS EC2).
  • PaaS (Platform as a Service): A platform allowing customers to develop, run, and manage applications (e.g., Heroku).
  • SaaS (Software as a Service): Software licensed on a subscription basis and centrally hosted (e.g., Google Workspace).

Deployment Models

Cloud deployment models include Public Cloud, Private Cloud, Hybrid Cloud, and Multi-Cloud.
