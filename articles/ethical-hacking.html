Ethical Hacking: Offensive Security

Security üìÖ Updated: December 2024 ‚è±Ô∏è 25 min read

Ethical hacking involves an authorized attempt to gain unauthorized access to a computer system, application, or data.

Introduction

Ethical hackers use the same methods and techniques as malicious hackers to find and fix security vulnerabilities before they can be exploited.

Phases of Hacking

  • Reconnaissance: Gathering information about the target.
  • Scanning: Identifying open ports and services.
  • Gaining Access: Exploiting vulnerabilities to enter the system.
  • Maintaining Access: Ensuring continued access to the system.
  • Clearing Tracks: Removing evidence of the hack.

Common Tools

Ethical hackers use a variety of tools, including Nmap, Metasploit, Burp Suite, and Wireshark.

Bash 
# A simple Nmap scan to find open ports
nmap -sV -T4 192.168.1.1
